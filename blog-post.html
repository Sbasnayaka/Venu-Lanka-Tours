Sigiriya Rock Fortress

Central Province, Sri Lanka Estimated reading: 4 min

Discover the Eighth Wonder of the World

Sigiriya Rock Fortress, also known as Lion Rock, is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most iconic landmarks in Sri Lanka. The fortress rises 200 meters above the surrounding jungle and offers breathtaking panoramic views.

A Brief History

Built by King Kashyapa during the 5th century AD, Sigiriya served as a royal palace and citadel. After the king's death, it became a Buddhist monastery until the 14th century. The site is famous for its ancient frescoes, mirror wall, and the massive lion's paws staircase that once led to the palace.

What to Expect

Visitors climb approximately 1,200 steps to reach the summit. Along the way, you'll see the famous Mirror Wall (once polished so brightly that the king could see his reflection) and the rock shelters with ancient graffiti. The water gardens, boulder gardens, and terraced gardens are marvels of ancient engineering.

Best Time to Visit

The dry season from January to April is ideal for climbing Sigiriya. Early morning (7–9 AM) offers cooler temperatures and fewer crowds. The sunset views from the top are spectacular.

Travel Tip: Wear comfortable hiking shoes, carry water, and start early to avoid midday heat.

How to Get There

Sigiriya is about 3–4 hours' drive from Colombo. The nearest town is Dambulla (15 km). Public buses and private taxis are available. Most tour operators, including Venu Lanka Tours, offer day trips from Kandy, Colombo, or Negombo.
